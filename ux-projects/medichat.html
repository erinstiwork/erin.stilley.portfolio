MediChat

Communication App for Patients and Health Care Workers
Client
University of Florida Design-A-Thon
role
UI/UX Designer
year
2024
Design

MediChat is a communication app that facilitates diverse connections and cultural exchange opportunities in a hospital setting. Patients can engage with fellow patients or non-patients, choosing from a variety of languages to communicate. The product was made under the theme: The Healthier Future.

Discovery

problems
There are pressing issues of mental health, boredom, and isolation among patients in hospitals. Recognizing the crucial need for support and connection, we set out to create innovative solutions that harness the power of technology to make a meaningful impact.
audience
We worked closely to define and prioritize the target audience for MediChat. Our goal was to depict the “who” of our audience, describe the similarities of users within a healthcare environment and highlight the differences between patients and healthcare workers.
questions

• How will this app create relationships between its users?

• What features do users need when learning languages?

• What will make communication engaging for patients and healthcare workers?

Design

user interface
The visual language of MediChat utilizes calm and tranquil colors and minimalist UI. Throughout the app, we wanted to reflect that identity and make sure our design elements were mobile-friendly and accessible. We designed styles for type, colors, buttons and icons.
key features

• Personal Messages: A page of personal messages with people the user is currently calling or texting with.

• User Profile Page: Each user has a profile page with their information. They can visit other profiles which highlight their similarities.

• Translator: Patients and healthcare workers can translate words and phrases as reference to learn their contacts' languages.

Testing

usability

After testing the app, we were able to highlight:

• Users appreciating its clean layout and intuitive navigation.

• Designing an intuitive app interface that promotes language literacy and cultural exchange.

• Implementing a virtual assistant to work with patients on specific needs.

Results

Working on this app with my team felt extremely rewarding. I was able to contribute to creating a tool that could work to support long-term patients. I learned a lot on this project:

• Understanding the complexities surrounding mental health support and the importance of leveraging technology to foster meaningful connections.

• Hone technical skills in natural language processing, bot development, and user interface design.

• Perseverance and teamwork are key to overcoming challenges in creating innovative solutions.
